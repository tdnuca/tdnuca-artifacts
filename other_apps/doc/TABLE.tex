      \midrule
      1. Finite State Mach       &\cm&\cm&\cm&\mm&\mm&\mm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\cm& A system whose behavior is defined by states. & Temporal data mining \\
      2. Combinational logic     &\cm&\xm&\lm&\xm&\lm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\cm& Functions that are implemented with logical functions and stored state. & CRC, encryption \\
      3. Graph Algorithms        &\cm&\mm&\mm&\mm&\cm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\lm&\lm& Visits many nodes in a graph by following successive edges. These applications typically involve many levels of indirection, and a relatively small amount of computation. & BFS, Bitonic sort, quicksort \\
      4. Structured Grids        &\cm&\cm&\xm&\mm&\xm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\xm& Represented by a regular grid; points on grid are conceptually updated together. It has high spatial locality. Updates may be in place or between 2 versions of the grid. The grid may be subdivided into finer grids in areas of interest (“Adaptive Mesh Refinement”). & SRAD, Cactus or latticeBoltzmann magnetohydrodynamics, Multi-Grid, Scalar Pentadiagonal \\
      5. Dense linear            &\cm&\cm&\mm&\cm&\cm&\cm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\cm&\cm&\xm& Dense matrices or vectors. (BLAS Level 1 = vector-vector; Level 2 = matrix-vector; and Level 3 = matrix-matrix.) Use unit-stride memory accesses to read data from rows, and strided accesses to read data from columns. & Block Triadiagonal Matrix, Lower Upper Symmetric Gauss-Seidel, LU Decomposition, BLAS or MATLAB \\
      6. Sparse linear           &\mm&\mm&\xm&\cm&\cm&\cm&\cm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\cm&\xm& Data sets include many zero values. Data is usually stored in compressed matrices to reduce the storage and bandwidth requirements to access all of the nonzero values. Because of the compressed formats, data is generally accessed with indexed loads and stores. & CG, Matrix multiplication, SpMV, OSKI, or SuperLU \\
      7. Spectral methods        &\mm&\xm&\xm&\mm&\mm&\mm&\cm&\xm&\lm&\cm&\cm&\cm& Data are in the frequency domain, as opposed to time or spatial domains. Use multiple butterfly stages, which combine multiply-add operations and a specific pattern of data permutation, with all-to-all communication for some stages and strictly local for others. & FFT \\
      8. Dynamic prog            &\mm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\mm&\xm&\cm& Computes a solution by solving simpler overlapping subproblems. Particularly useful in optimization problems with a large set of feasible solutions. & Needleman-Wunsch \\
      9. N-body methods          &\xm&\mm&\xm&\mm&\xm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm& Depends on interactions between many discrete points. Variations include particle-particle methods, where every point depends on all others, leading to an O(N2) calculation, and hierarchical particle methods, which combine forces or potentials from multiple points to reduce the computational complexity to O(N log N) or O(N). & GEM, RRU(LANL), Barnes-Hut, Fast Multipole Method \\
      10. Backtrack/B\&B         &\xm&\xm&\mm&\xm&\cm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\mm&\xm& Finds an optimal solution by recursively dividing the feasible region into subdomains, and then pruning subproblems that are suboptimal. & N-Queens, Travelling salesman \\
      11. Graphical Models       &\xm&\xm&\mm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\cm&\xm& Constructs graphs that represent random variables as nodes and conditional dependencies as edges. & NVIDIA HMM, Bayesian networks and Hidden Markov Models. \\
      12. Unstructured Grids     &\xm&\xm&\xm&\mm&\mm&\mm&\cm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\cm&\xm& An irregular grid where data point locations and connectivity of neighboring points must be explicit. The points on the grid are conceptually updated together. Updates typically involve multiple levels of memory reference indirection. & Unstructured Adaptive, CFD Solver, ABAQUS or FIDAP \\
      13. MapReduce              &\xm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\cm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm&\xm& Calculations depend on statistical results of repeated random trials. Considered embarrassingly parallel & Embarrassingly Parallel, PSICL-BLAST, Monte Carlo \\
